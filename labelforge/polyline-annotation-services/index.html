Use Cases for Polyline Annotation

Road markings

Drivers must ensure their own safety and others by paying heed to road signs and symbols. For self-driving vehicles, it helps in making them cognizant of the basic but most important road safety rules for preventing collisions. A know-how of road markings and symbols will help in preventing crash and ensuring a smooth ride.

Detecting lanes

Akin to road markings, self-driving vehicles must be capable of identifying lanes in the road like while the two lines in the center of the road divide traffic, the dotted white lines indicate a one-way. As car manufacturers become familiar with AI, drivers can derive benefits of technology that helps them in staying in their lane without drifting. Polyline annotation make it easy to detect the lane making it easy and most effective.

Detecting objects

As seasoned drivers, one is used to several obstacles on the road. However, autonomous vehicles must be taught to identify these hurdles to prevent accidents. Polyline annotation allows for a manner of labeling that displays these road signs without going into its details.

Robots are used in warehouses for lifting and moving objects from one point to another. They are used basically for restocking items as they are efficient, save company’s time, as well as money. So, polyline image annotation are a good fit here as it creates a target zone between the two lines for placing the objects.

FAQs

Polyline image annotation involves using linear structures in images and videos. It makes use of small lines which are linked at vertices for tracing the shape of structures including roads, rail tracks, and pipelines. This annotation technique is used by annotators for applying lines to images through annotation platforms and labeling tools.

1. Complex visual representations: This is a key challenge in polyline annotation. In case of intricate or densely packed designs, it is a must to ensure that the annotations are clear and do not cause visual clutter.

2. Selecting the apt annotation style: This can be a challenge as it needs to take into account factors like the purpose, the context, and the target audience. It is very important to strike a right balance between information and visually pleasing annotations.

3. Staying consistent: To create a cohesive and professional look, there needs to be consistency in annotation styles spanning different visuals. But it can be a challenge to achieve consistency when one is functioning with multiple teams.

4. Precise Placement and Alignment: Annotations need to be aligned in a proper way along points or segments. This requires great care and attention to detail. Any placement error can result in misinterpreting the visual content.
