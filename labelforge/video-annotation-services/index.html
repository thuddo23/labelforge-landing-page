Advanced Video Annotation Technology

We provide accurate and cost-effective video annotation solutions to streamline your business processes. Here’s a brief overview of our services:

2D Bounding Boxes

Draw rectangular boxes around objects in each video frame to accurately identify and categorize people or items.

3D Cuboid Annotation

Convert 2D video frames into a 3D perspective by drawing bounding boxes that outline objects, enhancing visual perception for AI models.

3D Point Cloud Annotation

Analyze 3D scenes to detect objects in space and track motion patterns, supporting advanced applications in machine learning and computer vision.

Landmark Annotation

Enable precise facial recognition by marking key facial features, ensuring detailed analysis for human-centric AI tasks.

Lines & Splines

Identify and track the starting and ending points of lines or curves, monitoring changes in direction, speed, and shape over time.

Polygons Annotation

Draw polygons around objects such as vehicles, people, and buildings to segment images and video content accurately.

Events Classification

Manually label video clips with tags related to specific events by drawing bounding boxes around objects, improving event detection accuracy.

Event Tracking

Develop an event tracking system that provides valuable insights into user behavior, such as monitoring the frequency of visits to particular locations.

FAQs

Video annotation involves detecting objects and events within video content and labeling them with specific markup using advanced video labeling tools. These labels are then utilized by machine learning algorithms to build models that extract meaningful insights.

For startups and SMBs, outsourcing video annotation is typically recommended. Outsourcing saves time and money while ensuring higher accuracy, whereas in-house annotation can be labor-intensive and slow.

A robust video annotation tool provides features such as precise timestamp annotation, shape markup, screen recording, frame-accurate feedback, and hassle-free sharing, all of which enhance collaboration and accuracy.

1. Dataset quality: Start with a clean dataset by removing low-quality and duplicate data before annotation.

2. Appropriate Labeling: Use the right labels (e.g., bounding boxes for object detection) to ensure data is ready for machine learning.

3. Organized Labels: Implement customized label structures and accurate metadata to avoid miss-classification.

4. interpolation and keyframes: Interpolation & Keyframes: Use keyframes and interpolation to reduce manual work on predictable motion segments, speeding up the process while maintaining quality.

5. User-friendly Tools: Choose robust, efficient annotation tools to create precise labels that improve training outcomes.

Video data is complex and high-dimensional, making it challenging for computer vision algorithms to accurately detect and label objects and events. Additionally, the dynamic and intricate nature of video content often requires human oversight to achieve a comprehensive understanding.
